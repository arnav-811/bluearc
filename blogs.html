TDS Rate Chart, FY 2026-27 (AY 2027-28)

A comprehensive TDS rate chart for FY 2026-27 covering all sections under the Income Tax Act. Know the applicable rates for salary, interest, rent, professional fees, and more.

Key TDS Rates at a Glance

Section Nature of Payment TDS Rate
192 Salary As per slab rates
194A Interest (other than securities) 10%
194C Payment to contractor / sub-contractor 1% (Individual/HUF) / 2% (Others)
194H Commission or brokerage 5%
194I Rent (land, building, furniture) 10%
194J Professional / technical fees 10% (2% for technical services)
194Q Purchase of goods 0.1%

TDS (Tax Deducted at Source) is a mechanism where tax is deducted at the point of payment itself. The deductor is responsible for deducting the applicable TDS and depositing it with the government within the stipulated time frame.

Non-compliance with TDS provisions can attract penalties, interest, and even disallowance of expenses. It is therefore critical for businesses to maintain accurate TDS records and file returns on time.

Capital Gains Planning – How to Save Tax Legally

Discover legal strategies to minimize capital gains tax on equity, mutual funds, and property. Learn about indexation, reinvestment exemptions, and timing your exits.

Strategy 1
Indexation Benefit
Adjust the purchase cost of assets for inflation using the Cost Inflation Index (CII) to reduce your taxable long-term capital gain.
Strategy 2
Section 54 Exemption
Reinvest gains from property sale into a new residential property within the prescribed time to claim full or partial capital gains exemption.
Strategy 3
Tax Harvesting
Systematically book long-term capital gains up to ₹1.25 lakh per year (tax-free for equity) to reset your cost basis and reduce future tax liability.
Strategy 4
Section 54EC Bonds
Invest up to ₹50 lakhs in specified bonds (NHAI, REC) within 6 months of sale to claim long-term capital gains exemption on property.

The key to effective capital gains planning is timing. By aligning your exits with the financial year and understanding the holding period requirements, you can significantly reduce your tax outgo while staying completely within the bounds of the law.

Long Term and Short Term Capital Gain on Various Income

Understand the distinction between LTCG and STCG across different asset classes — equities, debt funds, real estate, and gold — and how each is taxed under Indian law.

Asset Class STCG Holding Period STCG Tax Rate LTCG Holding Period LTCG Tax Rate
Listed Equity Shares < 12 months 20% ≥ 12 months 12.5% (above ₹1.25L)
Equity Mutual Funds < 12 months 20% ≥ 12 months 12.5% (above ₹1.25L)
Debt Mutual Funds < 24 months Slab rates ≥ 24 months Slab rates (no indexation)
Real Estate < 24 months Slab rates ≥ 24 months 12.5% (no indexation)
Gold / Other Assets < 24 months Slab rates ≥ 24 months 12.5%

Understanding the classification of your gains is the first step toward effective tax planning. The distinction between short-term and long-term is determined solely by the holding period of the asset — and getting it right can mean a significantly lower tax bill.
